About Gift of Gratitude

GOG is a charity that is hosted by a group of friends who joined together to make the people in Vancouver's Christmas memorable and joyful. They sell donuts during Winter season and all profits they will be using it to give and gift on Christmas. They usually giveaway foods like donuts, clothings, and more. This event is personally hosted by one of my COO's and I asked if I can develop the website for them to be able to gather more volunteer and donations.


The Process

Throughout three weeks, as a solo developer and designer. I listed out a sitemap and moodboard first to show it to the client and give them an initial idea what the website will look like. I also have done research and list out things needed in the website. Firstly, I made the wireframes so we can make changes before the development process. Then after, I started out developing the website.


Design Process


My COO's friend has already designed a logo therefore I made an inspiration from the colour of the logo they made to make a colour theme and typography. The logo they made is vibrant and bubbly which then I decided to make a sitemap and moodboard based from it, something that will look like fun and where the user will feel comfort.


Development Process


I focused on building a responsive, user-friendly platform using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. I planned to make the users be clarified easily about what the website is about and make the donation process as smooth as possible.
One of the challenges I have encountered was implementing a "Donate" button, as it required both ease of use and flexibility for the charity. After exploring various options, I decided to use Squarespace and a Google Form to offer a simple and secure way for users to make donations or join as a volunteer.
Aside from errors, content development was another challenge as the client did not provided the necessary information before the deadline. Despite this, I focused on creating a solid structure and design for the website, ensuring that content could be easily added later without affecting the overall layout and user experience.
